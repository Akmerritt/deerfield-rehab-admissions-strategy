# Staff insights: Call center staff and BDOs

**Overview:** Observations from interviews with stakeholders across the admissions funnel to identify operational friction and bottlenecks

## 1. Treatment specialists (Call Center)
*   **Role:** Handles patient intake, assessments, and coordinates care plans
*   **Process:** (1) Call to verify insurance details (2) Use Salesforce and Five9 to gather information about the patient including medical history and addition background (3) Intake asessment to understand the patient's needs and level of care (4) Develop care plan, ranging from detox to residential treatment or outpatient services. Use Microsoft teams and outlook to coordinate with the treatment team.
  
*   **Pain point 1:** High turnover
*   **Impact:** Disrupts tean dynamic and continuity of care; makes it hard to maintain consistent quality
  
*   **Pain point 2:** Long onboarding times for staff
*   **Impact:** Training new staff takes significant amount of time. Extended onboarding strains resources.
  
*   **Pain point 3:** Scattered tools:** Rely on various tools and systems, PDFs / Excel spreadsheets, which aren't integrated.
*   **Impact:** Inefficiences and errors when transferring between systems.

*   **Pain point 4 (Impact: patients drop out):** Initial contact, where patients or families reach out but don't follow through after the initial call
*   
*   **Pain point 5 (Impact: patients drop out):** Insurance verification and pre-authorization. If there are issues with insurance coverage or pre-authorizastion takes too long, patients may lose momentum or decide to explore other options.

*   **Pain point 6 (Impact: Patients drop out):** Pre0admission. Between schedluing and assessment and beginning treatment, patients may reconsider.
